## Overview

Welcome. This course exists to turn someone whose only experience of AI is typing into
ChatGPT into a confident **AI architect, operator, and decision-maker** — without requiring
you to become a programmer.

That last part is the whole point, so let's be precise about it.

```callout
title: What you're signing up for
- **9 tracks, 92 short lessons** (~10–13 minutes each). You do **not** need to finish everything to get value.
- **Fastest path to useful:** Orientation → Foundations lessons 1–4 → then the track for your job (Operations, Governance, Architecture…). Skim freely.
- **No coding, no setup, no math by hand.** When something needs building, you'll *direct* an AI tool — and we show you exactly how.
- **Free, no account.** Your progress saves in your browser; mark lessons complete to track where you are.
```

## Why this matters

There are two very different skills in the AI era:

1. **Building** AI systems — writing the code, training the models, wiring the infrastructure.
2. **Directing** AI systems — understanding how they work, choosing the right approach,
   governing the risk, and telling AI tools what to build.

Most courses teach the first. The market increasingly needs the second. As AI coding tools
(Claude, Cursor, Codex) get better at *building*, the scarce, valuable skill becomes knowing
**what to build, why, and how to keep it safe** — the architect's job, not the bricklayer's.

You can learn that without learning to lay bricks.

## Core concepts

**You will not run code in this course.** When something needs to be built — a RAG system, an
automation, a fine-tune — you'll learn the concept deeply enough to *specify* it and direct
an AI tool to implement it. We'll show you exactly how.

**We abstract technical concepts; we don't skip them.** You won't derive equations by hand.
But you *will* understand what a tensor is, why attention matters, when fine-tuning beats
retrieval, and how to reason about it all. Abstraction is not dumbing-down — it's learning at
the altitude a decision-maker actually operates at.

**Every lesson follows the same five-verb loop:**

> **Understand → Classify → Decide → Apply → Govern**

You understand the idea, learn where it sits in the ecosystem, get a framework for *when* to
use it, see how it applies in real businesses, and learn its governance implications.

## How it works

Each lesson has a consistent shape so you build a rhythm: an overview, why it matters, core
concepts, a visual, a **decision framework** (the most valuable part), common mistakes, real
business examples, **governance considerations**, a **"how an architect thinks"** reframe,
the **tools** in that category, and a copy-pasteable **"how to ask Claude / Cursor"** prompt.

You'll also see recurring callout cards:

```decision
title: How to read a decision card
When you see a card like this, it's giving you a rule for *choosing* — when to use something, when not to, and the trade-offs. These are the heart of the course. Screenshot them; reuse them at work.
```

```architect
"How an architect thinks" cards reframe a topic from "which tool?" to the questions that actually matter — scale, latency, cost, governance, quality. This is the mindset you're here to build.
```

## How to get the most from it

- **Go in order within a track, but tracks are independent pillars** — you can jump to
  Governance or Operations if that's your need. The recommended path is Orientation →
  Foundations → the rest.
- **Don't memorise; internalise the decisions.** Nobody will ask you to recite what a
  transformer is. They'll ask "should we build this with RAG or fine-tuning?" — and you'll
  have a framework.
- **Use the glossary.** Every term is defined in plain language. Look things up freely.
- **Mark lessons complete** to track your progress (it's stored only in your browser).
- **Try the prompts.** The fastest way to cement a lesson is to take its "ask Claude" prompt,
  adapt it to your own situation, and run it.

## Common mistakes

- **Thinking you need to code first.** You don't. The whole course is designed around that.
- **Skipping the "boring" foundations.** The Decide and Govern skills rest on understanding
  the concepts. Ten minutes on embeddings saves you from expensive mistakes later.
- **Treating it as trivia.** The value is judgment, not facts. Engage with the decision cards.

## Real business examples

- A **founder** uses the course to decide where AI actually pays off in their business — and
  to brief a developer (or an AI agent) precisely instead of hand-waving.
- A **consultant** uses the tool-classification and decision-framework lessons to advise
  clients credibly without pretending to be an ML engineer.
- A **lawyer or compliance lead** uses the Governance track to set safe AI policy for a firm.

## Key takeaways

- This course makes **AI directors, not AI bricklayers**.
- You'll **understand** deeply and **direct** AI tools to build — no coding required.
- The repeating spine is **Understand → Classify → Decide → Apply → Govern**.
- The **decision cards** and **architect notes** are where the real value lives.
